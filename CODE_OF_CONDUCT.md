# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Regrowth project is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free community for all contributors, users, and participants, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or socioeconomic status.

We believe in the power of collaborative restoration—both ecological and social. Just as permaculture works with natural systems to create abundance, we work together respectfully to build a game that celebrates regeneration and community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors We Encourage

- **Be respectful**: Treat others with kindness, empathy, and dignity
- **Be inclusive**: Welcome people with different backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives
- **Be constructive**: Offer feedback that helps others improve; focus on ideas, not personalities
- **Be patient**: Remember that contributors may be learning and have different time zones and commitments
- **Be collaborative**: Share knowledge, celebrate successes, and learn from challenges together
- **Assume good intent**: Interpret unclear communication charitably before escalating
- **Acknowledge mistakes**: If you make an error, apologize sincerely and seek to make it right
- **Amplify marginalized voices**: Actively create space for contributions from underrepresented groups in tech and gaming

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated in our community:

- **Harassment**: Unwelcome comments, jokes, or actions related to protected characteristics (race, gender, sexuality, disability, etc.)
- **Discrimination**: Exclusion or differential treatment based on identity or background
- **Intimidation**: Threats, targeted attacks, or aggressive language intended to silence or control others
- **Sexual harassment or abuse**: Unwanted sexual advances, comments, images, or contact
- **Doxxing**: Sharing someone's personal information without consent
- **Trolling**: Deliberately inflammatory or off-topic posts intended to provoke
- **Spam**: Repeated unsolicited promotion, advertising, or off-topic content
- **Bad faith arguments**: Arguing insincerely or in bad faith to derail constructive discussion
- **Gatekeeping**: Suggesting that someone isn't "real" enough, doesn't belong, or isn't qualified to participate

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces: GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, commits
- Project communications: Email, chat, video calls, forums
- In-person events: Game jams, conferences, meetups organized by or representing the project
- Interactions with other community members in project contexts
- Representing the project publicly (social media, presentations, etc.)

This Code of Conduct does not apply to:

- Private conversations unrelated to the project
- Interactions outside of project spaces or contexts

## Enforcement

### Reporting Incidents

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

1. **Email**: [contact@regrowth-game.org](mailto:contact@regrowth-game.org) (preferred for privacy)
2. **GitHub Issue** (private): Use GitHub's Report Abuse feature (if applicable)
3. **In-person**: Speak directly with a maintainer at project events

**When reporting**, please include:

- A description of what happened
- Who was involved (if comfortable sharing)
- When and where it occurred
- Any witnesses or evidence (if applicable)
- How the incident affected you or others
- What resolution you seek (if any)

**All reports will be:**

- Treated seriously and investigated promptly
- Kept confidential to the extent possible
- Handled with discretion and care for all parties

### Investigation & Response

1. **Initial Response** (within 48 hours): Acknowledge receipt of the report
2. **Assessment** (within 1 week): Investigate the incident, gather context, and interview involved parties
3. **Resolution** (within 2 weeks): 
   - Determine if the Code of Conduct was violated
   - Decide on appropriate consequences (see below)
   - Communicate decisions to the reporter and involved parties (privacy permitting)

### Consequences

Consequences for violations range from warning to permanent removal, depending on severity:

1. **Clarification & Warning**: For minor first-time violations, a private conversation clarifying community standards
2. **Temporary Suspension**: For repeated violations or moderate misconduct (48 hours to 30 days)
3. **Permanent Ban**: For severe harassment, harassment of minors, sexual abuse, or repeated violations after suspension

Decisions are made by the project maintainers and may be appealed through the same reporting channel.

## Creating a Welcoming Environment

### Tips for Reviewers & Maintainers

- **Frame feedback constructively**: "I'd suggest..." rather than "You should..."
- **Celebrate contributions**: Thank people for their work, even small improvements
- **Be patient with new contributors**: Remember your first projects were learning experiences too
- **Respond to confusion kindly**: Explain concepts without condescension
- **Acknowledge effort**: Recognize when someone is learning or working in good faith

### Tips for Contributors

- **Ask for help**: It's okay to say "I don't know" or "Can someone help me understand?"
- **Provide context**: When asking questions or reporting issues, share what you've already tried
- **Respect maintenance bandwidth**: Maintainers are volunteers; they will respond when they can
- **Follow style guides**: Adherence to CONTRIBUTING.md makes review faster and easier
- **Celebrate others**: Acknowledge good contributions and offer support to fellow contributors

## Special Considerations

### Accessibility & Inclusion

- Use inclusive language (avoid gendered pronouns unless someone prefers them)
- Provide alt text for images and captions for videos
- Ensure discussions are accessible to people with disabilities
- Be aware of different time zones and work schedules

### Gaming & Permaculture Communities

Given our dual focus on game design and permaculture:

- **Respect domain expertise**: Permaculture practitioners, ecologists, and game developers each bring valuable knowledge
- **Avoid gatekeeping**: Just because someone learns permaculture from a game doesn't make their interest less valid
- **Celebrate intersection**: The bridge between gaming and restoration is innovative and worth exploring together

## Updates to This Code

This Code of Conduct may be updated as the community grows and evolves. Updates will be announced clearly and applied prospectively. Community feedback on this Code is encouraged and appreciated.
